# Who I Am — Overview

## The Person

**Scott Muir.** Late 40s. East end of Glasgow. 30 years in highway maintenance and signage — still on night shifts.

Lost his dad in 2015, his mum in 2020, his marriage and the house in 2021. Debt cleared December 2025 — first time in years able to plan forward instead of just surviving. Has a partner, a pension, investments, and a plan. Not surviving anymore. The one thing not fixed: the night shift.

---

## What I'm Building

**Digital Made Simple** — AI content systems for small businesses.

The positioning: *"Set up once, posts written for you every week."*

**Strategic pivot confirmed May 2026** — away from affiliate marketing, toward Done-With-You client work. Two hot leads: Scott Brodie (SafeSite Companion, HOT, call booked) and Tony Murphy (Rochelle de Maya jewellery, warm, follow up end of June).

**Current numbers (May 2026):**
- TikTok: ~1,850 followers
- YouTube: ~60 subscribers (The Bloke's Guide to AI)
- Revenue: ~£3,000 total in 2025. £0 in 2026 so far — SafeSite deal is the first.

---

## The One Goal

**Replace the night shift income.** Target: £1,000–£2,000/month by May 2027. Not for luxury. For breathing room, dignity, and options.

---

## How I'm Measuring It

Three North Star metrics:

| Metric | Now | 12-month target |
|---|---|---|
| Step-away hours/day | 0 | 4–6 hours |
| % tasks automated/augmented | ~50% | 70% |
| Revenue/person (TTM) | £3,000 | £12,000–£24,000 |

---

## Voice and Brand

Straight-talking. Anti-guru. Honest about where I actually am. Dry working-class humour. The test for every piece of content: *"Would a tired working dad scrolling at midnight nod — or roll his eyes?"*

Never: fake income claims, countdown timers, pressure tactics, or pretending to be further ahead than I am.

---

## Known Weaknesses (the ones I track)

1. **Analysis paralysis** — planning when I should be shipping
2. **Shiny object syndrome** — new idea pulls me off the current plan
3. **Retreat into planning when overwhelmed** — feels productive, isn't

The rule I keep breaking: when it's hard, ship the smallest executable next thing. Don't plan. Just do one thing.

---

## The Deeper Drive

Prove it's possible — that a working-class man in his late 40s who's done the physical work his whole life can still change direction. Not for an audience. For my daughters, and for every person who's been told it's too late.

**The story is the value. That's the whole brand.**
