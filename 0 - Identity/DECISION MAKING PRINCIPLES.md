# DECISION MAKING PRINCIPLES

> The rules and filters you run every decision through.

## Core principles

**The deciding question:** Does this actually move me toward replacing the night shift, or does it just feel like progress?

This is the filter. Shiny ideas, new platforms, interesting courses — they all have to pass this test first. Most don't.

**When overwhelmed:** ship the smallest executable next thing. Don't plan. Don't research. Make one piece of content, send one DM, do one thing. Planning when overwhelmed is a trap I keep falling into — every time I do it, I lose momentum and it takes twice as long to get back.

**Strategy changes happen at the end of cycles, not in the middle.** If I'm questioning the plan mid-execution, I finish the cycle first, then evaluate. Changing direction mid-stream is almost always shiny object syndrome in disguise.

## Heuristics I trust

- If it requires pretending I'm further ahead than I am, it's not worth doing
- If I've been "planning" for three days without making a single piece of content, I'm stalling
- A new platform or channel doesn't get started until the current one is producing consistently
- Every "I should probably also do X" gets challenged — the default answer is no, not yet

## Biases I watch for

**Analysis paralysis / overthinking.** I can spiral into research when the answer is just to act. I notice it when I've been planning for days and shipped nothing.

**Shiny object syndrome.** A new idea comes and I'm pulled toward it instead of finishing what I started. Four years of on-and-off is partly this. The fix is someone (or a system) that calls it out and redirects to the current plan.

**Retreat into planning when overwhelmed.** The rule I keep breaking: when things feel hard, I plan instead of executing the next small thing. It feels productive. It isn't.
