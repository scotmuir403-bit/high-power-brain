# INTELLECTUAL BLUEPRINT

> How you think — the mental models, frameworks, and domains that shape your worldview.

## Mental models

**The tired-shift-worker test.** Before publishing anything: *"Would this genuinely connect with a tired working dad scrolling after a long shift — or would he roll his eyes and scroll past?"* If he'd roll his eyes, rewrite it. This model strips out bullshit instantly. It works because it's not hypothetical — I am that person.

**Does this move the needle.** Before starting anything new: *"Does this actually move me toward replacing the night shift, or does it just feel like progress?"* Kills shiny objects faster than any willpower.

## Core frameworks

**Build in public — honestly.** The anti-guru approach isn't just a marketing angle. It's the operating framework: show real progress, admit real failures, let the audience see you figure it out. No manufactured wins. No income screenshots. No countdown timers. The trust this builds is slower but real.

**Your story is the asset.** The night shift, the divorce, the losses, the on-and-off — not liabilities to overcome. The relatability IS the competitive advantage. Every person who tries to polish these away is making a mistake.

## Domains of mastery

**Highway maintenance and signage — 30 years.** Could teach anyone. The physical reality of road work, signage standards, shift culture, what the job does to your body over decades. No one in the online business space knows this world from the inside. That's rare.

**Building an online business as a working-class man in your late 40s — in real time.** Still learning. Learning by doing. The gap between course content and what it actually takes is something I understand personally.
