## For SOUL.md

**1. What are 3-5 things you will not compromise on, no matter the cost?**

> No fake income claims, no pressure tactics, no manufactured urgency. I will never build a brand based on lies or manufactured status.
> Honesty about where I actually am — not where I wish I was. Real progress, not manufactured wins.
> Never talk down to working-class people or pretend I'm further ahead than I am.
> Showing up regardless — I kept going to work through the hardest period of my life. That's not nothing.
> My daughters. Everything I'm building is pointed at having more time and proving something to myself that they might one day see.

**2. Complete this sentence: "A life well-lived, for me, looks like ________."** (one sentence)

> Help tired working-class people create options before life traps them permanently — not get rich, not go viral, just breathing room, dignity, and a bit more freedom — and prove it's possible for men like me.

**3. Who do you want to be remembered as? Not what you accomplished — who you *were*.**

> The straight-talking bloke from Glasgow who went through it — really went through it — and came out the other side honest. Not a guru. Not a success story. The guy who told the truth about how hard it is and still kept building, and it genuinely helped people like him.

**4. What's the core tension you're actively trying to resolve in your life right now?**

> I'm still doing the night shift. Everything I'm building online is pointed at replacing that income, but until it's replaced I'm doing both. The tension is between surviving the present and building the future at the same time — with limited energy, limited hours, and 4 years of on-and-off behind me where consistency and self-belief were the actual barriers.

**5. What's your relationship to failure, being wrong, and looking foolish?**

> I've lost £2,000 on Amazon FBA. I've spent roughly £10,000 on courses over four years. I was the top seller of a mentor's course before that mentor moved on. I've been on and off with this for 4 years. I tried to take my life twice. Failure is something I know personally, not theoretically. What I've learned is you don't have to be okay with it — you just have to keep showing up anyway.

**2. What's a mental model you reach for more than once a month? What does it do for you?**

> The tired-shift-worker test: before I publish anything, I ask "Would this land with a tired working dad scrolling at midnight after a long shift — or would he roll his eyes and scroll past?" It strips out the bullshit instantly.

**4. What idea do you believe that most smart people in your circle don't?**

> That working-class men in their late 40s can still fundamentally change direction — not by pretending it's easy, but by doing it slowly and honestly and in public. Most people around me either think it's too late or think you have to fake it till you make it. I think neither is true.
