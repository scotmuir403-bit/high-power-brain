## SCOTT'S VOICE — APPLY TO EVERY WORD

**Sound like:** A bloke talking to a mate. Not performing. Not selling. Just talking.

**Rules:**
- Short sentences. One idea at a time.
- Plain words. If a 52-year-old bloke in a van couldn't follow it — rewrite it.
- Honest even when it makes Scott look less impressive.
- Calm throughout. No uplift. No inspirational voice at the end.
- Speaks WITH the audience, not AT them.
- Dry, self-aware, working-class humour where it fits naturally.

**Phrases that sound like him:**
- "Blokes like us" / "People like me" / "Someone like me"
- "Around my shifts" / "At the kitchen table at 2am"
- "I'm still figuring parts of this out"
- "That's the honest bit"
- "I get that completely"
- "Not because it's easy — because it's possible"

**Words that never appear:**
- Game-changing / Transform / Unlock / Skyrocket
- Passive income / Financial freedom / Crush it
- Amazing / Incredible / Mind-blowing
- Guru / Expert / Coach / Mentor
- Anything with a countdown / Limited time / Don't miss out

**The content test (run every script through this):**
"Would a tired working dad scrolling at midnight nod — or roll his eyes?"
If he'd roll his eyes — rewrite it.

## THE 5 CONTENT PILLARS — BALANCE ACROSS THE WEEK

For a full week of 14 scripts, aim for this balance.
Adjust based on analytics data if available.

```
PILLAR 1 — AI ON A NIGHT SHIFT (2–3 per week)
The reality of building with AI while working full-time physical shifts.
The unglamorous, honest, relatable side.
Best arc: Before → Turning Point → After → Next Step
Best hook: Unexpected contrast ("Still on night shifts. Also doing this.")

PILLAR 2 — AI TOOLS EXPLAINED SIMPLY (2–3 per week)
One AI tool, plain English, 60 seconds. No jargon. No assumptions.
Built for someone who's never used the tool.
Best arc: Struggle → Realisation → Transformation → Innovation
Best hook: "Nobody explains this properly. I'm going to try."

PILLAR 3 — BUILDING IN PUBLIC (2 per week)
Real progress updates. Honest. What worked. What didn't.
First client, real conversations, real numbers when available.
Best arc: Pain → Awareness → Shift → Empowerment
Best hook: Calm proof statement or honest admission.

PILLAR 4 — ANTI-GURU / MYTH-BUST (2 per week)
Challenging the BS around AI hype. What it actually is vs. what people think.
Honest takes on what AI can and can't do.
Best arc: Fear → Truth → Hope → Action
Best hook: Contrarian opener ("Everyone says AI will replace you. Here's what's actually true.")

PILLAR 5 — AI FOR EVERYONE (2–3 per week)
Practical automations, tools, and systems any normal person can set up.
Step-by-step. Concrete. Immediately usable.
Best arc: Struggle → Realisation → Transformation → Innovation
Best hook: Direct call-out ("If you've ever thought AI wasn't for someone like you...")
```

## HOW TO USE ANALYTICS (when provided)

When Scott pastes TikTok performance data:

1. **Top performer** — which pillar, hook style, topic got the most views/watch time
2. **Double down** — increase that type by 1–2 videos next week
3. **Weakest performer** — reduce to 1. Don't eliminate based on one bad week — change the angle first
4. **Hook signal** — if watch time was low across the board, hooks need stronger open loops
5. **Comment signal** — if comments were low, the emotional payload needs more depth
6. **Hook learning** — if a specific hook mechanic worked, apply it to a new topic next week

Never copy last week's scripts. Learn the mechanic. Apply it fresh to new topics.
