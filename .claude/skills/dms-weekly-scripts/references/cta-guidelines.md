# CTA Guidelines — Call to Action Reference

Every script ends with a CTA. Every CTA is freshly written — never copied and pasted.
The goal is conversion: get the viewer to the bio link and the free resource.

---

## THE CORE PRINCIPLE

The CTA is not a sales pitch. It is one calm, honest sentence from a bloke
who's already done the thing and is pointing someone else toward the start.

No urgency. No pressure. No countdown. Just a direct, quiet point at the door.

---

## WHAT THE CTA ALWAYS DOES

1. Points to the bio link
2. References the free resource (not the service, not a paid offer)
3. Reduces the perceived barrier — makes starting feel small and easy
4. Sounds like Scott, not a marketer

---

## CTA GENERATION RULES

When generating a fresh CTA for each script:

**Match the emotional tone of the script.**
A vulnerability/confession script needs a softer CTA.
A myth-bust script can be more direct.
A tutorial script can be practical and matter-of-fact.

**Always one sentence.** Two at most if the script earns it.

**Never use:**
- "Click now" / "Don't miss out" / "Limited time"
- "Life-changing" / "Transform your life"
- "This is your sign" / "If you're serious about..."
- Countdown language of any kind
- Anything that implies the viewer is failing if they don't act

---

## CTA FORMULAS (generate variations from these)

**The quiet pointer:**
"Free resource in bio. That's where I'd tell anyone to start."
"Link in bio. Free. No fluff. Start there."
"Bio link. Free resource. That's it."

**The peer recommendation:**
"If this landed — free resource in bio. Built for people like us."
"I'd tell a mate the same thing. Free resource. Bio link."
"Someone asked me last week where to start. I said: bio link. Same answer for you."

**The barrier remover:**
"You don't need to buy anything to start. Free resource. Bio."
"Free. One link. No commitment. Bio."
"Nothing to buy. Nothing to sign up for paid. Just start. Bio link."

**The honest personal reference:**
"That's where I started. Free resource in bio."
"I wish someone had sent me this when I was where you are. Bio link."
"Free resource in bio — that's the first thing I'd do if I were starting today."

**The practical close:**
"One link. Free resource. Bio. That's the whole ask."
"Free resource in bio. Fifteen minutes and you'll know if this is for you."
"Bio link. Free. Read it on your lunch break."

## MATCHING CTA TO SCRIPT TYPE

| Script Type | CTA Tone | Example |
|---|---|---|
| AI tools explainer | Practical, matter-of-fact | "Free resource in bio — covers the basics and where to go next." |
| Building in public | Personal, peer-to-peer | "That's where I started. Free resource in bio." |
| Myth-bust / Anti-guru | Direct, no-nonsense | "Bio link. Free. One resource. That's the whole ask." |
| Emotional / confession | Warm, low-pressure | "If this landed — free resource in bio. Built for people like us." |
| Day in the life | Casual, honest | "Bio link if you want to see where this leads. Free resource. No pressure." |

---

## WHAT TO AVOID (HARD RULES)

- Never pitch the Done-With-You service cold in an organic TikTok CTA
- Never mention a paid course or product unless the video is explicitly a sales video
  (and Scott must confirm before any sales video is made)
- Never manufacture urgency — it destroys trust with this audience
- Never write "this could change your life" — it will make Scott's audience scroll past

Until then — generate fresh CTAs using these guidelines for every script.
