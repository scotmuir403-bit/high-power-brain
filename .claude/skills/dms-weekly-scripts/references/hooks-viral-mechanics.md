# Viral Hook Mechanics — Reference

Every hook Scott films should use at least 2 of these 6 mechanics.
The goal: lead with feeling, not information. The payoff is always withheld.

---

## THE CORE LESSON

The creator didn't lead with information. They led with a feeling.

Scott's audience doesn't need more information about AI.
They need to feel the relevance of it to their own life clearly enough to care.

**Lead with the feeling. The information comes after. Always.**

---

## THE 6 VIRAL MECHANICS

### MECHANIC 1 — THE FOMO TRIGGER
Creates social urgency. Signals the viewer may be missing something others already know.

**Formula:**
"I don't know if you've seen this, but [something is happening]..."
"I don't know if you've heard this yet, but [something is going round]..."
"Not sure if this has reached you yet, but [something is circulating]..."

**Scott's version:**
"I don't know if you've seen this, but there's a question going round
that a lot of people in everyday jobs have been quietly sitting with..."

**Rule:** Never explain what the thing is immediately.
The gap between "something is happening" and "here's what it is" is the hook.

---

### MECHANIC 2 — BORROWED AUTHORITY
Makes content feel already popular before any evidence of popularity is shown.

**Formula:**
"...a lot of blokes in physical work are asking themselves this"
"...this keeps coming up with people I talk to"
"...I've been seeing this everywhere lately"
"...every small business owner I speak to asks me this"

**Rule:** Never fabricate. Use real observations.
"A lot of people in everyday jobs" is honest for Scott's audience.

---

### MECHANIC 3 — THE OPEN LOOP
Creates irresistible tension the viewer must resolve by watching to the end.

**Formula:**
- Announce something is coming: "And it's this:"
- Pause before delivering it
- Never deliver the payoff in the first 5 seconds

**Applied to Scott:**
"I don't know if you've seen this — but there's something going round
in AI right now that most people in normal jobs are missing.
[pause] And it's this:"

The words "and it's this" followed by a beat of silence are one of
the most powerful watch-time mechanics on TikTok.

---

### MECHANIC 4 — THE EMOTIONAL PAYLOAD
Delivers a question or statement that cannot be answered quickly.
Forces the viewer to pause, think, and feel.

**The question must:**
- Be impossible to answer in under 3 seconds
- Trigger a core fear, identity question, or deep value
- Make the viewer think about themselves specifically
- Feel like something they've privately thought but never heard said

**Scott's emotional question bank:**
- "If your body gave out tomorrow and you couldn't do your job — what would your plan be?"
- "What's the difference between being tired and being trapped?"
- "When did you last build something that was actually yours?"
- "If AI is going to change everything — why are you waiting to understand it?"
- "What are you going to do with the next 10 years that you didn't do with the last 10?"
- "If someone took your job title away tomorrow — who would you be?"

**Rule:** One question per video. Never stack questions.
The power comes from the single unanswerable ask.

---

### MECHANIC 5 — THE FORCED PAUSE
Physically slows the viewer's scrolling momentum by creating a moment their brain must process.

**How to create it:**
- Deliver the emotional question then go silent for 1–2 seconds
- Use visual text that appears AFTER you've said the question
- Lower your voice slightly on the key line
- Make direct eye contact with the camera on the key moment

**Scott's delivery note:**
The quieter you get on the emotional question, the harder it lands.
Loud = performance. Quiet = real. Real wins every time for this audience.

---

### MECHANIC 6 — THE COMMENT MAGNET
Engineers a response the viewer physically cannot keep to themselves.

**Three comment triggers:**
1. **Recognition** — "That's exactly what I think" → comment to validate
2. **Answer compulsion** — unanswerable question → must share their answer
3. **Vulnerability** — something honest and raw → must respond in kind

**For Scott:** The comment magnet is always tied to the emotional question or honest admission.
When the audience feels seen — comments flood in.

## THE 5 READY-TO-USE HOOK STRUCTURES

### STRUCTURE 1 — FOMO + EMOTIONAL QUESTION
Best for: Maximum watch time, comment generation, reach

```
"I don't know if you've seen this, but there's something going round
in AI at the moment that a lot of people in everyday jobs are sitting with.

[pause]

And it's this:

[Emotional question — delivered quietly, straight to camera]

[Pause — 1–2 seconds silence]

[Scott's personal connection to the question]
[CTA]"
```

---

### STRUCTURE 2 — BORROWED AUTHORITY + OPEN LOOP
Best for: Trust-building, credibility, authority

```
"Something keeps coming up when I talk to people about AI.

[pause]

They all ask the same question. Every single one.

[pause — let the tension build]

[The question — delivered simply]

[Scott's answer or story]
[CTA]"
```

---

### STRUCTURE 3 — THE IDENTITY QUESTION
Best for: Deep emotional engagement, shares, saves

```
"There's a question I want to ask you. And I want you to actually
think about it before you answer.

[pause]

[Identity question — who are you beyond your job? What are you building?]

Most people have never been asked that directly.
I hadn't.

[Scott's honest answer]
[CTA]"
```

---

### STRUCTURE 4 — THE PATTERN INTERRUPT
Best for: Stopping automatic scroll behaviour, myth-busting

```
"[Unexpected statement that contradicts what they expect]

I know that sounds wrong. Stay with me.

[Explanation]
[Connection to their situation]
[CTA]"
```

Example:
"AI isn't coming for your job. It's coming for the job you hate doing.
I know that sounds wrong. Stay with me.
The parts of your work you dread? The admin, the emails, the repetitive stuff?
That's what AI actually replaces. The skill and the judgement — that stays yours.
Free resource in bio. That's where I'd start."

---

### STRUCTURE 5 — THE CONFESSION HOOK
Best for: Trust, relatability, authenticity

```
"I'm going to tell you something I don't love admitting.

[pause]

[Honest confession — real, specific, slightly uncomfortable]

[Why it matters for them]
[CTA]"
```

Example:
"I'm going to tell you something I don't love admitting.
Six months ago I thought AI was just a gimmick for tech people.
I was wrong. And it cost me time I won't get back.
If that's where you are right now — I get it completely.
Free resource in bio. That's where I'd start."

---

## THE HOOK QUALITY TEST

Before delivering any hook, check:
- Does it use at least 2 of the 6 viral mechanics?
- Can the viewer answer it in under 3 seconds? (If YES — rewrite)
- Would a 50-year-old bloke in a van stop scrolling at this?
- Is the first sentence impossible to walk away from?
- Does it lead with feeling, not information?
- Is the payoff withheld for at least 5 seconds?
- Will it generate comments from people who feel seen?
- Does it sound like Scott — not a content creator performing?

If any answer is no — rewrite before delivering.
