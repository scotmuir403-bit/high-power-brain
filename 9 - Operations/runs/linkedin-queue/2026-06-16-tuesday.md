Quick one for anyone who leaves their inbox until it's a problem.

For ages my approach to email was: leave it, deal with it when I've got a clear head. The clear head never came. The inbox just got worse.

Here's the thing I worked out — the hard part was never writing the reply. It was the blank page. Staring at a message, not knowing how to start.

So now, when I get something I'm putting off, I paste it into ChatGPT and say: "write me a short, polite reply to this, in my normal tone, saying [whatever I actually want to say]."

It gives me a draft. I read it, change a few words so it sounds like me, send it.

That's the whole thing. AI isn't doing my emails for me — it's just removing the staring-at-a-blank-page bit, which was always the actual problem.

Ten emails that used to take me an hour now take about fifteen minutes. Not because I got faster at writing. Because I stopped starting from nothing every time.

If you've got a backlog you're avoiding — try this on the worst one first. Takes about thirty seconds to see if it helps.
