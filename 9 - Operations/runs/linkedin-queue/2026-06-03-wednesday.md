Companies have replaced thousands of workers with AI in the last twelve months.

That's not speculation. It's on the record. And the people most affected aren't being spoken to clearly.

So here's my straightforward take, as someone who has a physical job and has spent two years learning these tools.

If your work is mostly processing information, writing, or answering the same questions repeatedly — AI is already doing versions of that. The displacement is real and it's happening now.

If your work involves skilled manual labour, physical presence, or complex judgment calls — you're safer than the headlines suggest. For now.

But the question most people are asking is the wrong one.

"Will AI take my job?" misses the point.

The better question: "Will someone who uses AI take my job?"

Because that's what's actually happening in every industry. The people moving ahead aren't being replaced by AI — they're using AI to do more, move faster, and become harder to replace.

I'm not saying this to scare anyone into buying a course. I have no course to sell.

I'm saying it because I started learning two years ago, while working nights, with no tech background — and it changed what I can offer, what I can earn, and what my options look like.

That's the real conversation.
