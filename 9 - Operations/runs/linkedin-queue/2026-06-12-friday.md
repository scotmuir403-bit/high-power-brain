Here are my actual numbers. No filter, no highlight reel.

Just under 1,900 followers on TikTok. Built up since March, posting consistently — the longest stretch I've managed since I started any of this.

No viral video. No big breakthrough moment I can point to. Just more weeks where I showed up than weeks where I didn't.

Around £3,000 in revenue across all of last year — most of it not even from this side of the business yet. I'm not saying that to impress anyone. I'm saying it because most people building something on the side never show you this part. Just the bit where it's already working.

The honest version is slower and less exciting than that. It's also, I think, more useful to actually see.

If you're thinking about starting something — a business, a page, a side project — and you're waiting for some sign that it's worth it before you begin: that sign might not come before you start. It tends to show up after, in hindsight, once you've put enough weeks together.

Start before you feel ready. That's the only advice I've got that's actually held up.
