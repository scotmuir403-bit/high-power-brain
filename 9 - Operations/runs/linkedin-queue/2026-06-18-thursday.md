ChatGPT passed a billion monthly users this week. A billion. That's not a niche tool for tech people anymore — that's just normal now.

A year ago I'd have put myself in the "not for someone like me" camp. Thirty years in highway maintenance, never written a line of code, no interest in "tech" as a thing.

The bit that's stuck with me isn't the number itself. It's where I think most of those billion people actually are when they're using it. Not all in offices, behind two monitors. A lot of them are probably like me — on a break, tired, trying to get one small thing sorted in ten minutes.

For small business owners, the headline isn't "AI is everywhere now, panic." It's closer to: this stuff has gone from "thing I should probably look into" to "thing most people are already quietly using" — and the gap between those two groups is mostly just whether someone's opened it and asked it one real question yet.

If you're in the first group — curious, haven't started — the bar to find out if it's useful for your business is genuinely about fifteen minutes. Not a course. Not a strategy session. One real question about one real problem.

Happy to point anyone toward where to start if that's useful.
