A while back I told a few people online I was building a website for my sister's dance school using AI. First real test of something I'd actually made for someone else.

She's run that school for ten years. Packed classes, trophies on the shelf, parents who've been with her since their kids were five. No website. Ever.

That's not because she's behind. It's because when you're running something day to day, there's never a clear week to "sort the website out properly." It just sits on the list forever.

I built her one in the gaps between night shifts. I'm not a web developer — never built a site before in my life. Didn't need to be.

This week she actually looked at it. No big reaction, no "this is amazing." Just: "that's exactly what I needed — when can people see it."

That's the bit that matters more than anything flashy. Something that was on a list for ten years is now real enough to show people.

It's not finished. But it's live, and it works.

If you've got something in your business sat in the "I'll do that properly one day" pile — that's usually the thing worth starting first, even badly.

Happy to talk through what that might look like for yours.
