End of week. Real numbers. No spin.

This week I:

- Posted 14 videos across TikTok. Every one, even on the three nights I was on shift.
- Held my first client discovery call. First serious conversation about building an AI content system for someone else's business.
- Posted 5 times on LinkedIn. One post turned into a proper DM conversation with a business owner. Worth more than likes.
- Grew to 1,895 TikTok followers. Modest. Consistent. Upward.

What didn't happen: a viral video. I keep waiting for that one. It'll come when it comes.

I started building this seriously around September 2024. I work nights. I don't have a big following. I didn't have a tech background.

What I had was: a clear problem I wanted to solve (replace night shift income with online income), a commitment to showing up consistently, and the discipline to actually learn the tools instead of just watching people talk about them.

This week I spoke to a potential client who might be the start of something real.

That's not a result of luck. That's a result of eighteen months of posting, learning, and building in public — even when the numbers were small and the motivation wasn't there.

If you're at the beginning of something similar — keep going. The compounding is real, it's just slow.
