Honest one to close the week.

Some nights, after a full shift, there's just nothing left. I sit down, phone in hand, meaning to write something or sort something for the business — and nothing happens. Not because I don't want to. Because there's nothing left to give it.

For a long time I felt a bit guilty about that, like it meant I wasn't serious enough about building this.

Here's where I've landed on it: that's not failure, that's just the actual cost of building something on top of a full-time physical job. Pretending it doesn't cost anything would be lying.

What I've stopped doing is trying to push through every single night. That's how people burn out completely and walk away from the whole thing.

What I do instead — a script written on a good night still exists on a bad one. The work doesn't need to happen every day to add up. It just needs to not stop entirely.

If you're running a business on top of something else that takes everything out of you some days — the days you can't show up for it don't undo the days you did. They're just part of it.

Have a good weekend. Back at it Monday.
