## WHAT CLAUDE ACTUALLY IS
### [CAM] 0:45–2:00

Claude is an AI assistant made by a company called Anthropic. You talk to it. It talks back. That's genuinely most of it.

Think of it like having a very capable colleague available around the clock — one who doesn't get tired, doesn't judge you for asking basic questions, and can turn around a decent piece of work in about ten seconds.

It's not magic. It makes mistakes. You still need to check what it produces. But for most everyday tasks — writing, planning, summarising, thinking things through — it's genuinely useful.

### [SCREEN — show claude.ai] 1:15

There's a free version. You go to claude.ai, you sign up with an email, and you're in. The free version is fine to start with. You'll hit usage limits after a while — it'll slow down or stop responding for a bit. The paid plan is about twenty dollars a month. Worth it once you're using it regularly, but don't pay anything until you've tested it for free first.

## THE BIGGEST BEGINNER MISTAKE
### [CAM] 7:00–8:30

### [TEXT: "The #1 mistake beginners make"]

Most people treat Claude like a search engine. Short questions. Vague prompts. One or two words.

They type something like: *"email about invoice"*

And they get something vague and generic back — and they think: "this isn't that useful."

Here's the thing. Claude isn't a search engine. It's more like a person. And if you walked up to a colleague and said "email about invoice" — they'd look at you like you'd lost the plot.

The more you give it, the better it performs.

### [SCREEN] 7:45

Let me show you the difference.

**Bad prompt:** *"Write an email about my invoice"*

[show result — generic, bland]

**Better prompt:** *"Write a polite but firm email chasing an unpaid invoice for £350, two weeks overdue, one reminder already sent, professional and short"*

[show result — specific, usable]

Same tool. Completely different output. The only difference is how much you told it.

### [CAM] 8:15

You don't need to learn to code. You don't need any special skills. You just need to get comfortable treating it like a conversation — not a search box. Tell it what you want, why you want it, and any details that matter.

That's it. That's the skill.
