# The 7-Factor Viral Content Framework

Every script must address all 7 factors. When one is missing, performance drops.
This isn't a checklist — it's a system. Each factor amplifies the others.

---

## FACTOR 1 — TOPIC (The Subniche)

Not the broad niche — one specific subtopic per video.

**Scott's niche:** AI tools and automation for everyday people.
**His subniches (pick one per video):**
- AI writing tools (ChatGPT, Claude, etc.)
- AI automation (Zapier, Make, n8n)
- AI content systems for small businesses
- Building a business around AI on a tight schedule
- AI for people with no tech background
- What AI actually can and can't do (myth-busting)
- Real-life AI workflows Scott uses himself
- The business of selling AI services

**Rule:** One subniche per video. Never two. Specificity beats breadth every time.

---

## FACTOR 2 — HOOKS (Verbal + Written + Visual)

Three hooks work together in the first 3 seconds.

**Verbal hook:** The opening spoken line. Uses viral mechanics (see hooks-viral-mechanics.md).
**Written hook:** On-screen text overlay. 6–10 words. Bold. Appears as Scott speaks.
**Visual hook:** What the viewer sees. Setting, expression, action. Real life only.

**The 5x outlier rule:** The best hooks achieve 5x views vs. follower count on small accounts.
If a hook couldn't do that — rewrite it.

**Visual hook options for Scott:**
- Direct to camera, plain background, natural light — classic, effective
- Showing the AI tool on screen while talking — adds proof
- Split screen (Scott talking / AI output) — high value for explainer content
- Scott in work clothes or real environment — builds relatability

---

## FACTOR 3 — VALUE (The Measurable Takeaway)

The viewer must be able to DO something specific after watching.
Fluffy value = low saves, low shares.

**Push for specificity:**
Bad: "You'll understand AI better."
Good: "You'll know exactly which free AI tool to use to automate your email responses."

**Value types for Scott's content:**
- Knowledge: They now understand something they didn't before
- Tool: They know about a specific AI tool and what it does
- System: They have a simple workflow they can implement today
- Mindset: A belief that was blocking them has been addressed honestly

**Rule:** Define the value before writing the script body.
If you can't say "after watching, the viewer can [specific thing]" — the value is unclear.

## FACTOR 4 — SCRIPT ANGLE (The Direction)

7 angles. One per video. The angle determines how the information flows.

| Angle | Best for | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Tutorial | Step-by-step how-to | "Here's how to set up your first AI automation in 10 minutes" |
| Comparison | This vs. that | "ChatGPT vs. Claude — which one should you actually use?" |
| Myth Bust | Challenging bad beliefs | "AI won't replace you. Here's what it actually replaces." |
| Do's vs. Don'ts | Common mistakes | "Stop prompting AI like a Google search. Do this instead." |
| Tip / Hack | Single actionable insight | "One AI prompt that saves me 3 hours every week" |
| Transformation | Before and after journey | "I automated my entire content system. Here's what that looks like." |
| Challenge | Scott tested something | "I ran my business on AI tools only for a week. Here's what happened." |

**Combine with journey frameworks:** The angle is the content direction.
The framework is the emotional journey. Both are needed.

---

## FACTOR 5 — CTA (Call to Action)

See references/cta-guidelines.md for full CTA generation rules.

**Always:** Bio link first. Free resource. Never pitch the service cold.
**Never:** "Click now", "Limited time", "Don't miss out", fake urgency.
**Tone:** Calm, direct, one sentence. Like a mate telling you where to start.

## FACTOR 6 — FORMAT (How to Film It)

11 proven filming formats. Choose the one that fits the script's angle and topic.

| Format | Description | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| Talking Head | Direct to camera, plain background | Personal story, emotional hooks, myth-busting |
| Screen Share | Phone/laptop screen visible, Scott narrates | Tool explainers, tutorials, how-tos |
| Split Screen | Two things side by side | Comparison videos, before/after |
| Multitasking | Talking while doing something | Day-in-the-life, AI on a night shift |
| Shot Changes | New angle every 3–5 seconds | High-energy tips, listicles |
| Text-Forward | Minimal speech, mainly on-screen text | When environment is noisy, commute content |
| Walking/Moving | Scott moving while talking | More casual, behind-the-scenes feel |
| Close-Up | Face fills frame | High emotion, confession hooks, vulnerability |
| Demo | Showing AI tool in action in real time | Tool explainers, tutorials |
| Reaction | Scott reacting to something on screen | Myth-busting, news-response content |
| Voiceover | Scott's voice over visuals or screen | When visual proof is more powerful than face |

**Default for Scott:** Talking head is his strongest format. Direct, calm, no distractions.
Use Screen Share for tool explainers. Use Multitasking for day-in-the-life content.

**Filming notes (apply to every video):**
- Phone propped at eye level or slightly above. Portrait mode.
- Face a window. Natural light always beats artificial.
- Plain wall or natural home setting. Real beats polished.
- One or two takes max — the slightly imperfect one usually wins.
- No ring light unless natural light genuinely isn't available.

## FACTOR 7 — EDITING STYLE

Consistency builds a recognisable brand. Every video should look like it came from the same creator.

**Scott's editing style:**
- On-screen text: White bold, appears as the hook is spoken
- Font: Clean, sans-serif. No decorative fonts.
- Colours: White text on natural background. No heavy graphics.
- Captions: Always on. Auto-generated and corrected.
- Music: Usually none. The silence and delivery are the atmosphere.
  If music: very low, ambient, non-distracting.
- Cuts: Simple. Cut on breath pauses. No jump cuts for the sake of it.
- B-roll: Only if it genuinely adds clarity (e.g. showing an AI tool).

**The rule:** If the editing draws attention to itself — it's too much.
Scott's delivery is the content. The editing serves it, doesn't compete with it.
